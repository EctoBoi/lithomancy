You hold in your hands an ancient
game of both skill and luck, played by
wizards of all ages – the game of
Lithomancy

The objective is simple. You and your
opponent compete with your 
Stones, conjuring Spells, Potions and
Charms until one of you conquers four
adjacent Turrets around the Tower. 
The first to do so wins the game.

The Tower 

Overall Layout
The mat is oriented for two players — Player One sits at the top, Player Two at the bottom. The centerpiece is a large circle (the "tower")
The circular area contains a grid of dots/circles arranged in rows across the interior — these represent spaces for Stones during gameplay. The ring itself has 8 Gem slots positioned around its perimeter, each angled inward, which are the Turrets

Player Areas
Player One (top):
A row of 6 circular gem slots along the top edge — the Gems Line

Player Two (bottom):
A matching Gems Line — a row of oval/circular slots along the bottom

WHAT ARE STONES ? 

Stones comprise the heart
of this game. Each is a coin-like
stone with a rune (shape) on one
side and a star on the other.

[Triangle,Square,Pentagon,Hexagon,Octagon]
[4-point star,5-point pentagram,8-point stellated octagon,9-point fissal enneagram,10-point stellated decagram]
[4,5,8,9,10]

By casting different combinations
of shape and stars with your
Stones you can create
various Spells, Potions and
Charms to battle your opponent.
In many ways, the Game of
Lithomancy is a lot like the old Muggle
game “Rock-Scissors-Paper” except
that in this case it is “Spells-Potions-Charms.”
You and your opponent start the
game with 5 Stones. These
5 Stones form your Casting Hand.
You throw your Casting Hand to
generate Spells, Potions or Charms.
Each turn you make is called a
cast. If either you or your opponent
fails to cast a Spell, Potion or
Charm after your first throw, you
may choose to cast any or all of
your Stones a second time. You are
each allowed two casts per turn. 

You have a Potion if you cast
4 shapes and 1 star on your Stones.

You have a Charm if you cast
4 stars and 1 shape on your Stones.

You have a Spell if your cast yields
shapes on 3 Stones and
stars on 2 Stones, 

You have a Bungle if your cast yields
shapes on 2 Stones and stars 
on 3 Stones. A cast of 5 shapes
or 5 stars is also a Bungle.
This is a failed cast.

Once you and your opponent
complete your casts, compare them
to determine who wins that turn:

Spell beats Potion
Potion beats Charm
Charm beats Spell
Anything beats a Bungle

If both players cast a Spell
add up the visible 2 star values
the higher total wins the cast.
Same total is a draw.

If both players cast a Potion
the higher visible star value wins the cast.
Same value is a draw.

If both players cast a Charm
the visible shape with the most sides 
wins the cast.
Same shape is a draw.

Draws call for a new round with simultaneous casts.

Depending on the winning cast, the
victor of that turn is granted
different powers. 

Winning Spell allows 
the winner to place a Gem 
on one of the Turrets surrounding 
the Tower. 
You will come to realize that Spells
are the most important cast in the
game. They are the only cast that
allows you to place Gems
on the Tower Mat, and placing
Gems in a row upon four adjacent
Turrets is the only way to win.

Potions allow you to swap one of your in-play
(already on a Turret) Gems 
with an adjacent Gem 
belonging to your opponent. 
This is a great way
to prevent your opponent from
getting four in a row.

Charms allow you to Knock your opponent’s
Turret Gem off its Turret and
back into its owner’s line.

REMEMBER: Once a player
succeeds in placing 4 Turret Gems™
side-by-side on 4 Turrets around the
Tower Mat, that player wins and
game is over.

SEQUENCE OF PLAY

1. Both players cast
their Stones.
2. Both players take their
Second cast if they choose
to, re-casting any or all of
the Stones in their Casting
Hand.
3. Compare the casts and
determine who won the turn.
4. The winner executes his
victory action.
5. Whoever won the turn casts
first on the following turn.

When it's time for re-cast,
both players much choose to re-cast or 
stay before the re-cast occurs.
